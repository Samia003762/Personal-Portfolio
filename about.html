How I work

During my work experience at UP3, I was told that all their employees took a personality test where they get colours and descriptions based on their answers. I completed the quiz and got Earth Green (37%), Cool Blue (26%), Sunshine Yellow (21%) and Fiery Red (16%). After reading the summary I understood why I got those results as it really reflected my personality. The main adjectives that came up were structured, analytical, cooperative, cheerful and purposeful. I enjoy taking different personality tests as I can see objectively how I am and how others might view me; I took the MBTI test a couple times and either got ISTJ or ISFJ, ISTJ is the Logistician who are known for being dependable, responsible, and logical, and they thrive in structured environments with clear goals. ISFJ’s are practical, detail-oriented, and committed to serving others, often preferring structure and traditional values. This sums up how I usually work including the fact that I enjoy both working with people and alone as there are merits of both.

My strengths and weaknesses

I believe my strengths are my patience and ability to manage myself in all aspects, which come from both my familial life and outside activities. Being the youngest of 5 with a minimum 6 year age gap and maximum 13, I matured quite young as I learnt a lot from my siblings. I am also keen on constant improvement so I am able to learn and adapt quickly whether it’s technical or general skills. Prioritising my tasks has also been something I’ve been doing since my A Levels, so I could balance my studies, work and other things I had to get done. My main weakness is presenting and difficulty saying no, which mostly can be improved with confidence and the degree to which I know the other person. Once I am comfortable with the person I will be more likely to present my ideas and say no. In both my Valcon placement and UP3 I had to present to a group of people, which helped me face my difficulties and also get tips on how to improve.
